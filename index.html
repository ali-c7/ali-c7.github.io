What I do

Structural Analysis and Design

Solving structures might just be my strange addiction! Throughout my undergraduate career, structural engineering was one of the branches of civil engineering which stood out the most. Over these three years of struggling with solid-mechanics and structural analysis courses, I have developed an intuition for structural behaviour which helps me do things such as quickly drawing bending moment diagrams and performing a quick approximate analysis to verify the results of my work. I am planning on taking a course in Finite Element Methods, but have already had exposure to it in past workterms. In addition, I work on automating the analysis by creating spreadsheets and scripts (see 'My Work' section). In terms of design, I have had exposure in a course for the design of timber materials. This course focused on the topics of flexure, compression, and connections in plywood construction using calculations, design aids and the latest CSA design standards. I intend on taking courses on steel, concrete, and structural system design in the future.

Software Development

Automation is one of my other services. In my free-time I enjoy creating generic calculators to solve specific engineering problems. A side project I did was creating a web-app to solve Mohr's circle stress analysis problems. I have also created a mini-structural analysis calculator, able to solve forces and displacements of 3-membered frames. These projects have inspired me to make a small business of creating webapps for educational purposes. In my past work-experience, I had the opportunity to create a software for a state of the art application between Structural Engineering and CT Imaging. The software was able to accept stacks of images from a CT scanner and transform those images into a 3D model used for finite element analysis. As I improve my skills in algorithms and artifical intelligence, I hope to further participate in innovative projects which merge disciplines together. Although I do have a passion for structural engineering, I do enjoy solving problems from other disciplines as well.

Machine Learning and Artifical Intelligence

In my past work experience, I had the opportunity of contributing to interdisciplinary research on a paper titled 'Towards smart and sustainable pavement structures in Canada' with the research team at CPATT. This involved creating multiple machine learning and AI models to explore which input/output variables and underlying algorithms yielded the highest accuracy. Attempting to merge two disciplines together is something I find fascinating and I aspire to implement it in my future research and work. The field of civil engineering is slowly increasing the involvement of AI in academia, however I also hope to see it implemented in the industry. My experience in machine learning is relatively new but contributing to the research paper helped me gain exposure to a wide variety of different algorithms and their implementation. The most common ones were Random Forests, Decision Tree, Support Vector Machine, Linear Regression, and Artificial Neural Networks. These algorithms are not only useful for research, but they are extremely useful for data analysis and predictions in any discipline.
